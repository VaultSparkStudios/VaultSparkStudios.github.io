The Signal

Voidfall is not a story about first contact.
It is a story about what second contact costs — and who pays it.

The universe has a boundary. Not a physical edge, but a threshold of recognition — a point at which what lies beyond decides whether to acknowledge what approaches. Something approached. Something was acknowledged.

What followed is recorded in six volumes. The first is called the Origin Sequence. It begins not with the crossing, but with the three who were already waiting when it arrived.

First Pages

The light arrived before the sound.

That is not how light works. She knew that. She had known it since she was nine years old, standing in a field with her father, watching a storm move in from the west. Sound lags. Light is instant. The physics are not negotiable.

And yet.

The light came first — a line of pale violet drawn across the sky above the facility, sharp-edged and perfectly still. Then, three full seconds later, the sound arrived: not a crash, not a boom, but something lower. A tone. A frequency she felt in her back teeth.

She stood at the observation window on Level 4 and watched the line fade, and did not reach for the radio, and did not press the emergency trigger, because she understood — with a certainty that was not hers and had not been there a moment before — that this had already been decided.
